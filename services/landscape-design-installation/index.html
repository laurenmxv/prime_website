What's Included

Design & Installation Services

From the first site visit to the last plant in the ground, we handle every phase of your landscape project. Our process is collaborative — you always know what's going in, where, and why — and our installation crews are the same team that has built hundreds of landscapes across Central Florida since 2025.

  • Design Consultation

    We start with a detailed site visit: measuring planting beds, assessing soil drainage, documenting sun and shade patterns throughout the day, and reviewing your existing irrigation layout. We discuss your style preferences, maintenance tolerance, budget, and any HOA guidelines. Commercial clients also review signage clearances, sight-line requirements, and property management approvals before anything goes on paper.

  • Florida-Adapted Plant Selection

    We build plant palettes around species that thrive in Central Florida without demanding constant intervention. Structural favorites include crape myrtle and ligustrum trees; hedge borders are built from dwarf Walter's viburnum, Indian hawthorn, and wax myrtle. For accent color we turn to bird of paradise, cordyline, ixora, and firebush. Groundcover gaps get asiatic jasmine or liriope for clean, weed-suppressing coverage. Every plant is selected for heat tolerance, pest resistance, and reduced water demand once established.

  • Hardscape Integration

    Plants alone don't make a landscape — structure does. We design bed edging, stepping stone paths, decorative boulders, low retaining walls, and entrance feature elements that give your landscape visual organization and year-round interest even when seasonal color rotates. All hardscape is planned with drainage in mind: a common failure point in Central Florida's heavy summer rainfall. Proper slope and material selection prevent erosion and standing water in bed areas.

  • Irrigation Planning

    New plantings need consistent moisture during the 60–90 day establishment period in Florida's heat, and efficient coverage ever after. We design drip or micro-spray zones for planting beds as part of every installation, integrating new zones into your existing irrigation system or installing a complete new system when needed. Every design is built to comply with St. Johns River Water Management District watering restrictions from the first day the system runs.

  • Professional Installation

    Our crews work efficiently and leave sites clean: beds are properly prepped and amended, plants are set at correct depth, mulch is applied at 2–3 inches (never piled against plant bases where it causes crown rot), and all materials are hauled away the same day. We photograph before and after every installation for your records. Large commercial projects are staged in phases to minimize disruption to tenants and daily operations.

  • Post-Install Care Plan

    A completed installation needs a successful establishment period to look the way it did on day one — indefinitely. We provide a written 90-day establishment schedule covering irrigation frequency by week, the timing of first fertilization, and what warning signs to watch for. We also offer ongoing maintenance contracts so the team that designed and installed your landscape keeps it performing year after year.

How We Work

From Concept to Completed Landscape

Most landscape projects go wrong because the design phase is rushed and plants are chosen from whatever's available at the supplier that week. We slow down up front so the installation goes right and the results hold up for years.

01

Site Assessment & Design

We visit your property, document existing conditions, and develop a design tailored to your site, budget, and goals. Residential projects get a scaled bed layout with plant list, material callouts, and irrigation zone notes. Commercial projects include a full planting plan drawing. You review and approve everything before a single plant is ordered or any ground is broken.

02

Material Sourcing & Scheduling

Once the design is approved, we source materials from Central Florida growers whenever possible — plants that have already acclimated to the regional climate perform better after installation than stock shipped from out of state. We schedule installation during optimal planting windows: most Florida landscaping installs best October through April, outside the peak summer heat and early hurricane season stress period.

03

Installation & Walkthrough

Our crews install your landscape and leave the site spotless. At completion we walk you through the finished project, demonstrate new irrigation zones, point out any plants that need extra attention during establishment, and answer all questions about ongoing care. Clients receive digital before-and-after photos for their records and can reference us any time during the 90-day establishment period with questions.

Why Orlando Property Owners Choose Us for Landscape Design

We Design for Central Florida, Not a Catalog

Many landscapers pull from the same generic plant list regardless of location. Central Florida has specific challenges — freeze events in January, hurricane winds from June through November, alkaline soil pH that stresses acid-loving plants, and summer humidity that promotes fungal disease on poorly chosen species. Every design we produce is built around what actually grows here with minimal intervention, which means your investment holds up long-term.

One Company, Start to Finish

Some landscape design firms hand off installation to a subcontractor who has never seen the drawings. We design and install with the same team, so there is no translation gap between what was planned and what gets put in the ground. After installation, we can transition directly into ongoing maintenance — giving you a single point of contact for the life of your landscape rather than managing multiple vendors.

Commercial Design Experience

We've designed and installed landscapes for HOA common areas, office park entrances, retail center frontages, and apartment community courtyards across Central Florida. We understand approval processes, signage clearances, high-traffic durability requirements, and the aesthetics HOA boards and property managers expect. Residential clients benefit from that same level of care and planning precision on every project, regardless of size.
