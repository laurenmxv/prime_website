What's Included

Tree & Shrub Services

Central Florida's urban forest is diverse — sabal palms, live oaks, crape myrtles, magnolias, and dozens of tropical species all require different care approaches. Our crews are trained to prune correctly for each species, respecting natural growth habits rather than creating the topped, hat-racked silhouettes that plague so many Florida properties.

  • Tree Pruning

    Proper tree pruning in Florida balances aesthetics, structural integrity, and storm preparation. We remove crossing, rubbing, and codominant limbs that are structural failure risks; thin the canopy to improve wind resistance ahead of hurricane season; lift the canopy to maintain sightlines, clearance over parking areas and walkways, and airflow at ground level. All cuts are made at the branch collar using clean, sharp equipment to minimize disease entry points.

  • Palm Trimming

    Florida's state tree is the sabal palm, and Central Florida landscapes are full of them — along with queen palms, foxtail palms, and Mexican fan palms. Proper palm trimming removes dead, yellow, and damaged fronds while leaving all green fronds intact: over-pruning palms into a "hurricane cut" silhouette starves the palm of nutrients stored in green fronds and increases transplant shock risk. We also remove spent seed stalks and flower bracts that attract pests and create debris.

  • Shrub Shaping & Maintenance

    Hedges, foundation plantings, and ornamental shrubs need regular shaping to look intentional rather than overgrown. We shape Indian hawthorn, wax myrtle, viburnum, ligustrum, and other common Florida hedge plants to defined profiles — flat tops, rounded forms, or natural layered shapes depending on what fits the landscape design. We time cuts to avoid damaging new growth or removing emerging flower buds on blooming species like ixora and plumbago.

  • Hurricane Prep Trimming

    The recommended window for pre-hurricane tree trimming in Florida is before June 1 — well ahead of peak storm season. This allows any pruning wounds to callus over before stress events and gives time to address structural problems identified during the trim. We remove deadwood, reduce sail area in large canopy trees, eliminate overhanging limbs above rooftops and vehicles, and identify trees that may need professional arborist assessment for deeper structural issues.

  • Deadwood Removal

    Dead branches are a liability in Florida — they break in afternoon thunderstorms, drop onto roofs, and become entry points for wood-boring insects and fungal pathogens that spread into living tissue. We remove deadwood from landscape trees on a proactive basis as part of scheduled maintenance programs, rather than waiting for a post-storm call. Commercial property managers particularly appreciate deadwood removal over parking areas and pedestrian paths.

  • Canopy Lifting for Commercial Sites

    Commercial properties need clearance for visibility, pedestrian safety, and signage. Parking lot islands, entrance medians, and streetscape trees often grow to obscure signage or create low-hanging limbs over drive lanes. We lift canopies to a consistent clearance height, removing lower limbs back to the branch collar and balancing the overall tree crown so the result looks intentional rather than stripped. This work can usually be combined with your regular landscape maintenance schedule.

How We Work

Our Tree & Shrub Care Process

We approach tree and shrub care as a scheduled, proactive part of property maintenance — not just something that gets called in after a storm or when things look obviously bad. Regular attention keeps trees structurally sound, shrubs looking designed, and your liability exposure low.

01

Property Walk & Assessment

We walk your property, inventory the trees and major shrubs, and identify what needs immediate attention versus what should be scheduled. We flag structural concerns, overhanging limbs, deadwood, and shrubs that have outgrown their intended space. Commercial clients receive a written assessment with prioritized recommendations; residential clients get a verbal summary during the walkthrough.

02

Scheduled Pruning Program

Most Central Florida properties benefit from tree and shrub care on a semi-annual schedule — once in spring before summer growth flush, and once in late fall after the growing season slows. We build tree and shrub care into your regular maintenance schedule so it happens automatically, rather than becoming a reactive emergency after the first storm knocks a limb through your fence.

03

Cleanup & Debris Removal

All pruning debris — fronds, limbs, brush, and palm seed stalks — is removed from the property the same day. We never leave piles at the curb for "later pickup." Commercial clients with large volumes of material receive a precise timeline for debris removal so you're not managing a pile of fronds in your parking lot for a week after service.

Why Orlando Property Owners Trust Us With Their Trees

We Prune to Protect Tree Health

Crape murder — topping crape myrtles to knobby stubs — is unfortunately still common in Central Florida. It weakens trees structurally, invites disease, and ruins the natural vase form that makes crape myrtles beautiful. We refuse to top crape myrtles or any other tree for that matter, and we explain our reasoning to every client who asks. Proper pruning protects your tree's long-term value, which in a mature urban tree can be thousands of dollars.

Hurricane Season Expertise

We've been maintaining Central Florida properties through hurricane seasons since 2025. We know the timing window for pre-storm preparation, we know which tree species are most susceptible to wind failure (queen palms and eucalyptus top the list), and we know how to prioritize post-storm cleanup efficiently when multiple properties need attention simultaneously. If a storm is approaching, we can expedite pre-season trimming for our active maintenance clients.

Scaled for Commercial Properties

A 300-unit HOA community might have 200 palms, 50 crape myrtles, and several mature live oaks that all need attention before hurricane season. We have the crew size, the equipment, and the scheduling systems to tackle large commercial projects efficiently — without the delays that come from a small operation trying to punch above its weight class. Licensed, insured, and experienced with large-scale commercial tree and shrub care across Central Florida.
