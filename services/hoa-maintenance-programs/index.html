HOA Maintenance Programs

HOA Maintenance Programs in Orlando, FL

HOA landscape maintenance isn't just about cutting grass — it's about protecting property values, meeting community standards, and giving the board reliable documentation that the landscape contractor is performing. We serve commercial and HOA properties across Central Florida with custom monthly programs, dedicated crews, digital reporting, and the accountability that property managers and board members actually need.

What's Included

Complete HOA Landscape Maintenance

Our HOA programs are built around what communities actually need: consistent quality, documented proof of service, proactive communication, and a contractor that shows up the same day every week with the same team. No excuses, no surprises on the invoice, and no calling around to follow up on missed visits.

  • Custom Monthly Contracts

    Every HOA property is different — acreage, common area configuration, turf type, irrigation complexity, and aesthetic standards all vary. We build custom monthly service agreements that define exactly what is included at each visit, the frequency of each service, annual enhancement inclusions like mulch refreshes and seasonal color rotations, and the process for requesting additional work outside of contract scope. No ambiguity at invoice time means no disputes at board meetings.

  • Same-Crew Assignment

    Crew consistency matters more than most boards realize. A crew that has been servicing your community for months knows where the hidden irrigation heads are, which areas drain slowly, which beds have been recently planted and need lighter mowing around, and which resident's car is always parked in the spot that blocks the edger path on Thursdays. We assign dedicated crews to our HOA properties and minimize rotation — because institutional knowledge is what separates consistent quality from hit-or-miss results.

  • Digital Photo Reports After Every Visit

    We photograph common areas, entrance features, and any problem areas after every service visit and deliver a digital report to the property manager or board contact. This documentation serves multiple purposes: it gives the board objective evidence of service completion and quality, it creates a visual record of landscape condition over time, and it provides supporting documentation if questions ever arise about what was done on a given date. No other landscaping company in our market provides this level of transparency as standard.

  • Entrance Feature & Common Area Maintenance

    HOA entrance features — monument signs, fountain beds, specimen plantings, seasonal color displays — are the most visible part of your community's landscape and the first impression residents and visitors receive. We maintain entrance features to a higher standard than typical common area turf, with more frequent edging, detailed bed maintenance, and priority scheduling for seasonal color rotations. Entrance beds are always included in our digital photo reports so the board can verify condition without a site visit.

  • Irrigation Management

    HOA community irrigation systems are often large, complex, and inadequately documented. We take over irrigation management as part of our HOA programs: seasonal programming adjustments, regular head inspections to catch problems before they kill turf sections, leak detection and repair, and controller upgrades where appropriate. We track all irrigation work in the service record so the board has a complete history of system maintenance — valuable for planning capital expenditures and demonstrating SJRWMD compliance.

  • Seasonal Color Programs

    Coordinated seasonal color across all common areas, entrance features, and community facility beds creates the kind of polished, intentional appearance that distinguishes a well-managed community. We plan and execute two to four color rotations per year depending on your community's standards, selecting species appropriate for Central Florida's seasonal conditions — heat-tolerant pentas and vinca for summer, cool-season impatiens and snapdragons for winter — and installing community-wide on a single efficient visit to maintain a consistent palette throughout.

How We Work

Onboarding a New HOA Community

Transitioning an HOA community to a new landscape contractor is a significant undertaking. We manage the process methodically to minimize service disruption and give the board complete confidence in the handoff.

01

Community Assessment & Proposal

We walk the entire property with your property manager or board representative: measuring common area acreage, documenting the irrigation system layout, identifying enhancement needs, and assessing current landscape health. We ask about past contractor pain points and what the board's priorities are for the first year of service. Our proposal is detailed and itemized so the board can evaluate it against your current service for a true apples-to-apples comparison.

02

Contract Execution & Crew Assignment

Once the contract is executed, we assign a dedicated crew to your community and schedule a pre-service walkthrough with the crew lead. The crew lead meets your property manager, walks the entire property, and creates a site-specific service checklist that documents property quirks, problem areas, access notes, and board priorities. This briefing document stays with the crew and is updated as conditions change.

03

Ongoing Communication & Board Support

Your property manager receives a single point of contact — an account manager who is available by phone and email and responds to non-emergency requests within one business day. We attend board meetings when requested, provide annual landscape condition reports, and proactively flag capital expenditure needs (irrigation system components approaching end of life, specimen trees with structural concerns, turf sections that need renovation) so the board can budget appropriately rather than face emergency decisions.
