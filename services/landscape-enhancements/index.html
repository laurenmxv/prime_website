What's Included

Enhancement Services for Central Florida Properties

Landscape enhancements aren't a single service — they're a category of upgrades that refresh and improve your existing landscape. Whether you want color all year, a material upgrade in your beds, or a small sod repair after a dry spell, we handle the details.

  • Mulch Installation

    Fresh mulch is the single most impactful enhancement a landscape bed can receive — it suppresses weeds, retains soil moisture during dry season, moderates soil temperature, and creates a clean backdrop that makes plants pop visually. We offer pine bark, cypress blend, and eucalyptus mulch for organic beds, and rubber mulch for high-traffic commercial areas that need a permanent solution. We apply at 2–3 inches depth, never piling against plant or tree bases where it causes crown rot. Annual mulch refreshes are included in our HOA and commercial contracts.

  • Bed Edging

    Sharp, defined edges between turf and planting beds are a hallmark of a professionally maintained property. Over time, St. Augustine grass — which spreads aggressively by stolons — encroaches into beds and blurs the border. We re-cut bed edges with steel-blade edgers to restore clean lines, then edge regularly to maintain them. For properties that want a permanent edge solution, we install metal or concrete landscape edging to define bed borders without ongoing re-cutting.

  • Seasonal Color Rotations

    Florida's year-round growing season allows for true four-season color — but the species change dramatically between summer and winter. Summer rotations feature heat-tolerant, sun-loving annuals: pentas, vinca, marigolds, blue salvia, and torenia. Fall and winter transitions bring impatiens (in shade), snapdragons, petunias, and dianthus that thrive in our mild winters. We schedule color rotations as a planned service — typically twice a year — so your entrance features and focal beds never look tired. HOA properties especially benefit from coordinated community-wide color programs that create a cohesive, polished appearance.

  • Ornamental Plantings

    Adding a few well-chosen accent plants can completely transform a bed that's been running the same groundcover for years. We source and install ornamental grasses like muhly grass and liriope, tropical accents like bromeliads and agave, flowering perennials like coreopsis and salvia coccinea, and structural specimens like sago palms and dwarf yaupon holly. Every ornamental recommendation is matched to your sun exposure, soil drainage, and irrigation coverage so the plant thrives long after installation day.

  • Decorative Rock & Gravel Beds

    For high-heat, drought-prone areas — south-facing exposures, narrow strips between buildings, or utility zones — decorative rock and gravel beds provide a clean, low-maintenance alternative to organic mulch that requires regular replacement. We offer river rock, lava rock, pea gravel, and crushed granite in various sizes and colors. All rock installations include weed barrier fabric underneath and defined edging to keep material contained — because loose rock that drifts into turf areas or onto pavement creates its own maintenance problem.

  • Sod Installation & Repair

    Thin, patchy turf from drought stress, shade creep, pest damage, or high foot traffic can be repaired with targeted sod installation. We match your existing grass species — St. Augustine (Floratam or Palmetto), Zoysia, or Bahia — install on properly prepared soil, and provide an establishment irrigation schedule to ensure the new sod roots in fully. For larger areas, we install complete sod replacements and can coordinate with our irrigation team to ensure the new turf has adequate coverage during establishment.

How We Work

Planning Your Landscape Enhancements

Enhancements work best when they're planned, not reactive. We build enhancement schedules — mulch refresh dates, color rotation timing, bed re-edge frequency — into our maintenance contracts so these upgrades happen automatically before your landscape looks tired.

01

Assessment & Recommendations

We walk your property and assess the current state of your beds, mulch depth, color areas, and turf. We note areas that would benefit most from enhancement and provide written recommendations with approximate costs so you can prioritize. One-time enhancement quotes and annual enhancement programs are both available.

02

Material Selection & Scheduling

We help you select mulch type, color plant varieties, and any ornamentals based on your site conditions, aesthetic goals, and HOA guidelines. Seasonal color timing is planned around Florida's optimal planting windows so your annuals are at peak performance when they matter most — spring and fall, when properties receive the most traffic and attention.

03

Installation & Cleanup

Enhancement crews arrive with all materials and work efficiently without disrupting regular landscape maintenance schedules. Beds are prepped before any plants or mulch are installed — weeds pulled, old color removed, edges re-cut if needed. All packaging, plant containers, and debris are removed the same day. Commercial clients receive digital before-and-after photos after each enhancement visit.

Why Orlando Properties Choose Us for Landscape Enhancements

We Know What Grows in Central Florida

Annual color selection in Florida is nothing like the rest of the country. Impatiens that thrive in October die in June. Pentas that look spectacular in full summer sun burn out over a Chicago winter. We know which species perform in each season of a Florida year, which varieties are resistant to the fungal diseases that thrive in our humidity, and which plants give commercial properties the consistent, reliable color that HOA boards and property managers depend on.

Community-Wide Programs for HOAs

HOA communities benefit from coordinated enhancement programs that cover all common areas — entrances, medians, retention pond surroundings, and community facility beds — on a unified schedule. We manage multi-building and multi-phase HOA communities with a single point of contact, documented service records, and consistent color palettes across all common areas. Enhancement programs can be built into your annual HOA maintenance contract.

Proactive, Not Reactive

The most common enhancement mistake is waiting until mulch has faded and decomposed, color has died out, and beds look neglected before doing anything. We build scheduled refreshes into your maintenance plan with calendar reminders and automatic scheduling so your property always looks intentional — not like it's catching up. For commercial clients managing multiple properties, we track enhancement schedules across all locations.
