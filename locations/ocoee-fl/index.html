Ocoee Property & Landscape Notes

Ocoee's lakefront setting is one of its defining features. Properties along Starke Lake, Lake Bennet, and Lake Olympia require landscaping approaches that account for shoreline moisture gradients, occasional flooding, and the visual expectations of waterfront living. Sodded banks, native plantings near the water's edge, and proper grading all play a role in keeping these properties looking their best year-round.

The SR-429 Western Beltway has transformed Ocoee into one of the most convenient commuter locations in the metro, and that access has fueled a building boom of new single-family subdivisions and commercial projects around the Ocoee Town Center development. New construction landscaping in these areas means working with recently graded, compacted soil that often needs amendment before sod and plantings can truly establish. Our crews are experienced with the startup phase — starter fertilizers, frequent irrigation checks, and vigilant weed control during that first year.

Ocoee's older neighborhoods — the 1980s and 1990s subdivisions that line the city's interior — often have mature oak canopies that create both beauty and challenge. Large surface roots can complicate mowing near tree lines, and the shade cast by live oaks and Laurel oaks limits turf options. We recommend shade-tolerant grasses and ground cover blends in these situations, and our team knows how to edge cleanly around root-heavy areas without damaging the trees.

Commercial properties along Maguire Road, Clarke Road, and in the Ocoee Town Center district need to project a polished, professional image to attract the steady traffic that the SR-429 interchange has brought to the area. We provide scheduled commercial maintenance contracts that keep storefronts, parking lot perimeters, and entrance landscaping consistently clean — rain or shine, week in and week out.
