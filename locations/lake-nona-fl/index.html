Lake Nona Landscaping Considerations

As a master-planned community still actively under development, Lake Nona presents a distinct set of landscaping needs compared to older Central Florida neighborhoods.

Much of Lake Nona sits on soils that were former agricultural or wetland land — sandy, fast-draining, and low in organic matter. New construction sites in particular often have soil that's been graded, compacted, and stripped of topsoil, which makes establishing turf and plant material a challenge in the first few years. We account for this with proper soil prep, starter fertilization programs, and appropriate irrigation scheduling during the establishment phase.

Because so much of the community is new construction, irrigation is a major service need. Builders often install basic systems that need to be properly programmed, zoned, and adjusted after residents move in. We handle new system installs for custom homes, irrigation audits and repairs on builder-installed systems, and smart controller upgrades that tie into Florida-friendly watering schedules.

HOA requirements in Laureate Park and Eagle Creek are detailed and actively enforced. These communities were designed with a cohesive streetscape in mind, and landscaping that deviates from established standards can result in notices. We're familiar with these requirements and work with property managers and homeowners to keep compliance issues from becoming recurring problems.

The commercial district along Lake Nona Boulevard — including the USTA National Campus, medical facilities, and the Town Center retail area — requires grounds maintenance that fits a professional campus environment. Manicured turf, clean bed edges, and consistent appearance are non-negotiable for these high-profile properties.
