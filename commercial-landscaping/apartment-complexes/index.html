What We Provide for Apartment Complexes

Landscaping That Wins Leases and Keeps Residents Renewing

Every area of a multifamily property carries a different visual weight. We prioritize accordingly — your leasing office entrance gets the most attention, your dog walk turf stays healthy, and your pool deck never looks like an afterthought.

Leasing Office & Entrance Landscaping

The leasing office approach is your highest-ROI landscaping zone. We keep beds tightly edged, color rotated seasonally, and specimen plants in peak condition. Prospective residents judge the entire community by this 50-foot stretch. We treat it accordingly — fresh mulch, crisp edging, and seasonal annuals that make the property look actively cared-for rather than on auto-pilot.

Pool Deck & Amenity Area Maintenance

Pool areas, grilling stations, and fitness court surrounds take constant foot traffic and need constant attention. We trim ornamental grasses and shrubs around pool decks without creating clippings that end up in the water, maintain turf around outdoor fitness areas, and keep planting beds flanking amenity buildings clean and professional year-round. These are the spaces residents photograph and post — they matter for online reputation too.

Dog Park Turf & Dog Walk Areas

Dog parks are a top amenity driver for multifamily leasing and one of the hardest areas to keep looking good. High pet traffic compacts soil, burns turf with nitrogen, and creates bare patches fast. We aerate and overseed dog park turf on a scheduled rotation, maintain perimeter fencing plantings, and keep the entry gate surrounds groomed. A healthy dog park signals to pet-owning prospects that the management team pays attention.

Irrigation System Management

Multifamily properties typically run large, zone-heavy irrigation systems that were installed during construction and rarely updated. We audit controller programming, check and replace broken heads throughout the property, and adjust zone run times seasonally to match Florida's rainfall patterns. A properly programmed system on a multifamily property can cut water bills meaningfully and prevent the dry patches and soggy spots that generate resident complaints to management.

Seasonal Color Programs

Rotating annuals into beds at key visual touchpoints — leasing office entrance, monument sign, clubhouse entry — signals to prospects and residents that the property is well-managed. We handle the design, sourcing, installation, and removal of seasonal color programs timed to lease-up seasons and Florida's planting calendar. Impatiens in October through April, heat-tolerant vinca and pentas through summer — the right plant at the right time.

Pre-Hurricane Tree Risk Assessment

Florida insurance carriers and property management companies increasingly require documented tree risk assessments before hurricane season. We walk multifamily properties each spring and identify co-dominant leaders, included bark unions, root-lifting hazards, and species with known wind-failure risk. We provide written documentation and can handle prescribed pruning or removal before June 1. Protecting residents, reducing liability, and satisfying your insurance requirements.

Why Property Managers Choose Prime for Their Multifamily Portfolio

The number one complaint we hear from property managers who switch to us is that their previous contractor sent a different crew every other week — sometimes a crew that had never set foot on the property before. Nobody knew where the irrigation shutoffs were. Nobody knew the tree that had been flagged for pruning. The beds were inconsistent from building to building. Residents noticed, and they said so in reviews. Same-crew consistency is not a small thing in multifamily landscaping. When the same team walks your property every week, they know every head that's spraying sideways, every shrub that needs to be cut back before it blocks a window, every area that tends to wash out after a heavy rain. That institutional knowledge compounds over a contract year and shows up in the condition of your grounds.

We work with property managers and asset managers across Orlando's multifamily corridors — Lake Nona's new-construction communities, the Class A complexes along International Drive, the value-add properties in the Millennia Mall area and along South OBT. We understand that your contract landscape maintenance scope is typically itemized by building and common area, that digital service reporting matters for your records, and that a missed service or a no-show crew creates a management headache that no low bid is worth absorbing. Our commercial contracts include digital service logs you can pull at any time, a dedicated point of contact for your portfolio, and straightforward communication when a task falls outside scope rather than a surprise on your invoice.

We also know the seasonal pressures that drive multifamily leasing decisions in Central Florida. Spring lease-up season means the grounds need to look their absolute best from February through May. Pre-hurricane season means tree risk documentation in April or May. Summer heat means irrigation programming adjustments and turf stress management. Fall means transitioning out summer annuals before they look tired. We build these rhythms into our maintenance calendar so you don't have to chase us — the work gets done at the right time, and you have documentation to show your ownership group.
